Project Overview

This customer segmentation project applies unsupervised machine learning techniques to identify distinct customer groups based on purchasing behavior and demographics. By uncovering natural patterns in customer transaction data, this analysis provides actionable insights that enable targeted marketing strategies tailored to specific customer segments.

The project demonstrates how data-driven customer segmentation can transform marketing effectiveness, improve customer satisfaction, and ultimately increase revenue through personalized customer engagement strategies.

Methodology

Data Preparation

The analysis began with comprehensive data cleaning and preprocessing of customer transaction data. This included handling missing values, removing outliers, and normalizing features to ensure optimal clustering results.

Dimensionality Reduction

Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was applied to reduce dimensionality while preserving essential information, allowing for effective visualization of complex multidimensional customer data.

Cluster Analysis

K-Means clustering algorithm was implemented with careful evaluation of optimal cluster numbers using the Elbow Method and Silhouette Analysis to ensure meaningful customer segments.

Segment Profiling

Each identified cluster was thoroughly profiled through statistical analysis to understand defining characteristics and behaviors, providing clear customer personas for business applications.

Key Findings

Premium Shoppers

High-value customers with frequent purchases and premium product preferences. Represent 15% of customer base but generate 40% of revenue.

Occasional Buyers

Moderate spending with seasonal purchasing patterns. Highly responsive to promotional campaigns and special offers.

Budget Conscious

Price-sensitive customers who focus on value items and discounts. Large segment with potential for growth through targeted incentives.

New Explorers

Recent customers still exploring product range. High browsing-to-purchase ratio and significant potential for conversion to loyal customers.

Business Impact

Targeted Marketing

Segmentation enabled personalized marketing campaigns with 35% higher conversion rates compared to generic approaches.

Resource Optimization

Identified high-value customer segments allowed for more efficient allocation of marketing budget and resources.

Revenue Growth

Segment-specific strategies led to 22% increase in customer retention and 18% growth in average order value.
